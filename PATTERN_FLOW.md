# N86EC Pattern Flow

Companion to `pattern.png`. Everything that needs to happen from setup-to-shutdown in the traffic pattern, with the sources called out.

> Speeds without a citation are common Cherokee 140 practice, derived to stay above 1.3 × Vso on final and below Vfe when flaps come out. Anything marked **POH** is verified against the printed manual pages in `pages/`.

---

## Before entering the pattern

Well before you're at TPA — ideally as you begin descent from cruise.

- **ATIS / AWOS** — runway, wind, altimeter, ceiling, visibility
- **Fuel selector** → **fullest tank** (POH: don't use lowest tank on takeoff or landing)
- **Mixture** → **RICH** as you come through ~3000 ft density altitude
- **Landing light** → **ON**
- **Doors and windows** → verify **latched**
- **Seat belts and shoulder harness** → tight
- **Radio call** at 10 miles or per field procedure

At a towered field: contact tower on approach frequency, obtain squawk/runway assignment. At non-towered: monitor CTAF for at least the last 10 miles, first call at ~10 miles inbound.

---

## Downwind (level at TPA)

**Target: 90 MPH · Flaps UP · ~2200 RPM · TPA 1000 ft AGL**

Run the landing flow — I use **FMS**:

- **F — Fuel**
  - Selector on fullest tank (confirm)
  - **Electric fuel pump ON**  
    *POH Section II (page 51):* *"The electric fuel pump must be on for both takeoff and landing."*
- **M — Mixture**
  - RICH (already done, verify)
- **S — Seatbelts**
  - Verify tight

**Radio call:** *"[Field] traffic, N86EC left downwind [runway], full stop, [field]."*

Cherokee 140's fixed-pitch prop means RPM to hold 90 MPH level varies with density altitude and weight — **use whatever throttle setting holds 90 in level flight**, don't chase a specific RPM number.

## Abeam the numbers — the trigger point

This is where descent begins and configuration changes start. **Do them in this order:**

1. **Carburetor heat → ON**  
   *Before* pulling throttle. Carb heat drops RPM 50–100 already, so pulling throttle first double-counts the power reduction.
2. **Throttle → ~1700 RPM**  
   Enough to descend at pattern rate without shock-cooling the engine.
3. **Flaps → 1st notch (10°)**  
   As speed passes below 100 MPH (well under Vfe 115).
4. **Begin descent, trim for 80 MPH.**

That's four items in about 15 seconds of real time. Practice the flow until it's automatic — it happens fast at 90 MPH ground speed.

## Turning base

**Target: 80 MPH · Flaps 25° (2nd notch) · ~1500–1700 RPM**

- **Clear final** for traffic *before* rolling into the turn
- **Second notch of flaps** — 25°
- **Trim for 80 MPH**
- **Adjust throttle** to hit the glidepath
- **Radio:** *"turning left base"* (non-towered) or with tower instruction

If you're low, add power and knock off a notch of flaps. If you're high, throttle back further or slip (but not with full flaps in a Cherokee — read the manual on that; slips with full flaps can cause pitch oscillation on some PA-28s).

---

## Turning final

**Target: 75 MPH · Full flaps (40°) · ~1500 RPM (adjust)**

- **Third/full notch of flaps** (40°) — if the runway is long enough. On a short runway or gusty conditions, second notch is legit.
- **Trim** to 75 MPH hands-off. This is the key skill on final: trim so you can take your hands off the yoke and the airspeed stays put.
- **Pitch controls airspeed. Throttle controls glidepath.** In the pattern where you're glide-slope-managing constantly, this discipline matters.
- **Radio:** *"final [runway], full stop"*
- **Landing checklist verify:** carb heat still ON, fuel pump ON, mixture RICH, harness tight.

75 MPH is the POH-published approach speed for flaps-down (page 8-5). Verify against your specific POH page.

## Short final — landing assured

Once you're committed to the landing (typically inside 200 ft AGL over the fence):

- **Carburetor heat → COLD**  
  Two reasons:
  1. If you need to go around, full power is only available with carb heat OFF
  2. Carb heat pulls from the unfiltered alternate air intake near the ground — don't want dust/debris
- **70 MPH over the fence** — approximately 1.27 × Vso. Slim margin, don't get slow.
- **Fuel pump stays ON** through touchdown and rollout.
- **Throttle** → progressively to idle in the flare.
- **Flare** — pitch to arrest descent, hold nose off, touch down mains first.

---

## After landing — clear of the runway

Do these once you're **clear of the runway hold-short lines**, not while rolling on the runway.

- **Flaps → UP** (Johnson bar release, lever down)
- **Carburetor heat → COLD** (if not already)
- **Electric fuel pump → OFF**
- **Landing light → OFF**
- **Transponder → STBY** (if terminating flight; otherwise leave in ALT for taxi)
- **Taxi normally** to parking

---

## Shutdown

- **Idle at 1000 RPM for at least 1 minute** to let CHTs cool
- **Mixture → IDLE CUT-OFF** to stop the engine
- **Magnetos → OFF** (after prop stops)
- **Master switch → OFF**
- **Fuel selector → LEFT** (or as your practice dictates)
- **Control lock → INSTALLED**
- **Chocks / tie-downs** as needed

---

## ⚠ Go-around — any time

If the approach doesn't look right, go around. No shame in it, and a Cherokee 140 climbs fine even with full flaps hanging out — briefly.

1. **Throttle → FULL** smoothly, not slammed
2. **Carburetor heat → COLD** immediately (you need full power available)
3. **Pitch for Vy (85 MPH)** and stop the descent
4. **Flaps → UP in stages** — first notch out to reduce drag, then let the airplane climb before pulling the next
5. **Positive rate of climb confirmed** → announce ("N86EC going around"), sidestep the runway to the right, re-enter downwind

**Cherokee 140 gotcha:** the manual Johnson-bar flaps mean you can't quickly "milk them up" with one hand while flying with the other. Practice the coordination — release lever, lower to first notch position, latch, take a breath, next notch. The airplane is climbing at a reduced rate the whole time you have flaps out.

## Radio calls (non-towered, left pattern, runway 36)

Rough script — adapt to your actual field:

- **10 miles**: *"Grosse Ile traffic, Cherokee eight-six-echo-charlie, one-zero miles south, inbound landing, three-six, Grosse Ile."*
- **Entering downwind**: *"Grosse Ile traffic, eight-six-echo-charlie, left downwind three-six, full stop, Grosse Ile."*
- **Turning base**: *"eight-six-echo-charlie, turning left base three-six, Grosse Ile."*
- **Turning final**: *"eight-six-echo-charlie, final three-six, full stop, Grosse Ile."*
- **Clear of runway**: *"eight-six-echo-charlie, clear of three-six, taxi to parking, Grosse Ile."*
